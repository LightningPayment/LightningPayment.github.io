Our Mission


We strive to create a world where people can make point of sale transactions without having to
fumble around with physical objects such as cash, cards and cell phones.


We are working towards two key goals:

1. Reduce the need for merchants to hand out physical change
and for consumers to have to carry loose change with them.

2. Eliminate the need for a traditional wallet or a phone, in
that anyone can make a transaction at any time using simply
their biometric identity.


Secure biometric-authorized payments are the future, and we want to make the future safe and
easy for you to operate. Eventually larger corporations may enter this space, but we want to
make it so that your privacy is the top priority, not profits.




What We Do


We build biometric payment terminals that utilize facial and fingerprint recognition technology
to authorize transactions.

Using our terminals, people can opt to transfer any physical change that they receive on cash
transactions in the form of a cash balance. This balance can then be used on future transactions.

Most Importantly, our terminals allow people to link their preferred credit and debit cards as
well as any bank accounts to our online system during registration. In this way, people can make
transactions using any payment method on the go without carrying around any sort of wallet.

FAQ


“But what if someone takes a picture of my face or gets my prints and steals my money?”

Well, that’s actually easier said than done, since facial recognition technology usually
requires a 3D figure with a certain degree of height and depth as opposed to that of a 2D
image! However, in the event that someone was able to take a 3D mold of your face and
use it to steal your money, they would also require your PIN in order to make any
purchases. Given this, our biometric identification system is less dangerous than
getting your card stolen.


“Isn’t linking people’s biometric data to their bank accounts kind of dangerous?”

We don’t sell your data to anyone. Not the government, not to data brokers, not to private
individuals or companies. The only thing we use it for is to authenticate your identity
when you make purchases. All your information is encrypted, so in the event someone illegally
broke into our servers and took your info, it would not be readable, and couldn’t be linked
back to you.


“Is your goal to move us to a cashless society?”

No. This is not our goal at all. This is just a tool for people who feel extremely awkward
holding up a line while they fumble for their wallet in trying to look for their cards or change.


“What if I don’t feel comfortable having a camera pointed at my face while
I’m paying for things?”

We are planning to have an agreement with stores using our products that our terminal
is only to be pointed at you after you consent to using our product. The biometric
scanner would only be presented to you with your permission, and only then would you be
able to access your account. We do not keep records of your Biometric ID when you make purchases.
We only know the amount you spent so that you can make sure your account is secure and not being
maliciously misused. We only keep the scans you use to register your account with for reverse
authentication (your data is encrypted, of course).
